I worked very closely with engineers on all of the squads that would consume the design system to ensure that it was meeting their needs and that the process of introducing the design system to the pre-existing engineering ecosystem.

At Wonderbly (formerly Lost My Name), I was instrumental in being part of a highly motivated team who rebuilt their website from the ground up, taking it from a Rails-powered stack, to a performant React / Redux / Express application.

I have worked on many key architectural tasks, such as migrations towards a more declaratively-declared approach to powering our components, as opposed to a page-based approach which gave us huge gains in flexibility throughout the stack.

I have gained thorough knowledge of a full JavaScript stack, from settings up performant server-side code, middleware, and API management, through to performance-led frontend functional React code.

As a hybrid designer/engineer, I am able to troubleshoot and solve complicated code-based problems, but also be able to work effortlessly with Design teams to implement and enhance their work.

I also single-handedly created the Design System in the summer of 2016, which was well received throughout the company, but also by engineers at Airbnb and Tumblr amongst others.

The Wonderbly design system and website pre-dates the vast majority of design system frameworks that exist today, such as Storybook and Styleguidist, and pioneered the use of live, reactive, responsive components within a design system.

During April 2017, the company rebranded, and I was the frontend lead for delivering the web arm of the rebrand effort. Thankfully due to the work that had gone in to creating the Design System, this task was greatly simplified, as almost everything could be created and distributed through a centralised repository to all our applications.
